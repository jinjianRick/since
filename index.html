Less is More: Shrinking Injection to Reconcile Fidelity and Editability in Customized Generation

Jian Jin, Shangbing Gao, Zhenyong Fu, Jian Yang
IEEE Transactions on Multimedia
Paper

Previous customized generation methods inject customized information into the denoising backbone across all denoising steps and model layers (i.e., "full injection"), leading to an additional reduction in editability. SInCE effectively restores editability while preserving key identifying features of the concept, harmonizing concept fidelity and concept editability in customized generation.

Abstract

Customized text-to-image generation aims to render user-specified concepts into novel contexts as instructed by textual prompts. However, it suffers from an inherent trade-off between concept fidelity and editability, i.e., between the precise rendition of the customized concept and the faithful expression of the contextual content. In this paper, we exploit the nature of the diffusion model that handles different image content at varying denoising steps and model layers for fidelity–editability harmonization. Our initial analysis reveals that contextual content has narrow and consistent trigger intervals, where skipping the injection of customized elements into the denoising model effectively restores editability and mitigates overfitting. Leveraging the insights above, we propose Shrinking Injection of Customized Element (SInCE), a novel "subtractive" framework. In the fine-tuning, SInCE encapsulates all customized elements into a plug-and-play module using an adversarial tuning strategy. Besides, it modulates the trigger intervals of custom concept to better disentangle them from the intervals of contextual content. At inference, customized information is injected into the denoising model only outside the trigger interval of contextual content. The disentanglement between the trigger intervals of customized and contextual content enables SInCE to effectively restore editability without degrading concept fidelity. Extensive experiments validate the effectiveness of SInCE in harmonizing the fidelity–editability trade-off.

Method

we propose Shrinking Injection of Customized Element (SInCE), a novel subtractive framework, to address the inherent fidelity–editability tradeoff in customized generation. The core idea of SInCE is to exploit the temporal and spatial disentanglement between the trigger intervals of different image content. During finetuning, SInCE encapsulates all customized elements into a compact and plug-and-play module, enabling flexible control over the selective injection of customized information at inference. Besides, SInCE can modulate the trigger intervals of customized concept at fine-tuning to better disentangle them from the intervals of contextual content. At inference, SInCE skips injecting customized information at the trigger interval of contextual content to restore its normal expression, while injecting customized information in the remaining intervals is sufficient to trigger concept generation without degrading fidelity. Therefore, SInCE can restore contextual content while preserving high concept fidelity.

An overview of the proposed SINCE. In the fine-tuning stage, SINCE encapsulates all customized elements into a compact, plug-and-play module $\Phi_c$. SINCE can also modulate the custom trigger interval $T_c$ by adjusting the injection interval of $f_{fuse}$. At inference, customized information contained in $f_c$ is injected into $\epsilon_\theta$ only outside the contextual trigger interval $T_e$.
